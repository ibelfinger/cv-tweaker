# Discipline template: Generic (non-engineer fallback)

setup-from-cv uses this when the person is not clearly an engineer (PM, TPM, designer,
data, ops, marketing, ...). Discipline-neutral; adaptive follow-ups go deep on the person's
actual craft.

## Profile headline framing
A short professional headline in the person's own field, e.g. "Senior Product Manager",
"Technical Program Manager", "Lead Product Designer".

## about-me.md sections (in order)
- Professional identity - what kind of practitioner you are in your field, strengths, what you gravitate to.
- Leadership & collaboration identity - style, how you grow/work with people, how you decide.
- Craft & process beliefs - convictions about how the work should be done in your field (quality vs speed, team shape, rituals/methods, decision-making).
- Values & motivations - what you optimize for, what energizes you, what you won't compromise on.
- Working style & preferences - ambiguity tolerance, team size, remote/onsite, where you do your best work.
- Ways of working (detailed - pullable source) - longer-form concrete material cover letters/summaries can quote.
- Outside work - interests, side projects, communities.
- What's next - what you want in the next role (direction, not job-specific).

## role file (<role>.md) sections (in order)
- Narrative arc - why joined, state on arrival, how it evolved, why left.
- Context - what the org is, its scale, the business, your mandate.
- What I owned - teams, domains, budgets, surfaces, products.
- Initiatives - per initiative: situation/problem; what you did (decisions, approach, leadership moves); tools & methods used; outcome with hard metrics.
- Metrics bank - every quantified result (before/after, %, $, throughput, time).
- Tools, systems & domain - concrete tools, platforms, methods, and domain knowledge.
- Leadership & people - hiring, mentoring, stakeholders, who you reported to.
- Decisions & tradeoffs - judgment calls worth remembering.
- Challenges & lessons - what went wrong, what you'd do differently.
- Skills & keywords - flat tag list for ATS keyword matching.
- CV handling note - anything specific about how this role should/should not appear.
- Open threads - what is still thin.

## Interview coverage palette (deepen-role)
1. Narrative arc  2. Scope & scale  3. Key initiatives  4. Metrics
5. Tools, methods & domain  6. Leadership & people  7. Decisions & tradeoffs  8. Challenges & lessons
