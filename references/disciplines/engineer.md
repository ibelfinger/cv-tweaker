# Discipline template: Engineer (frozen)

setup-from-cv uses this to shape the workspace inventory when the person is an engineer or
engineering leader. Use these structures verbatim - do not improvise.

## Profile headline framing
A short professional headline, e.g. "Engineering Leader", "Staff Backend Engineer",
"Principal Engineer".

## about-me.md sections (in order)
- Engineer identity - kind of engineer (hands-on / architect / pragmatist), strengths, what you gravitate to.
- Leadership identity - style, how you grow people, how you decide.
- Dev-process beliefs - convictions about how software should be built (TDD, trunk-based, team shape, quality vs velocity, tech debt, incidents, metrics, decision-making).
- Values & motivations - what you optimize for, what energizes you, what you won't compromise on.
- Working style & preferences - ambiguity tolerance, team size, remote/onsite, where you do your best work.
- Leadership & ways of working (detailed - pullable source) - longer-form concrete material cover letters/summaries can quote.
- Outside work - interests, side projects, communities.
- What's next - what you want in the next role (direction, not job-specific).

## role file (<role>.md) sections (in order)
- Narrative arc - why joined, state on arrival, how it evolved, why left.
- Context - what the company is, its scale, the business, your mandate.
- What I owned - teams, domains, budgets, surfaces.
- Initiatives - per initiative: situation/problem; what you did (decisions, architecture, leadership moves); tech & tools used; outcome with hard metrics.
- Metrics bank - every quantified result (before/after, %, $, throughput, time).
- Tech & architecture - concrete languages, frameworks, datastores, infra, patterns.
- Leadership & people - hiring, mentoring, stakeholders, who you reported to.
- Decisions & tradeoffs - judgment calls worth remembering.
- Challenges & lessons - what went wrong, what you'd do differently.
- Tech & keywords - flat tag list for ATS keyword matching.
- CV handling note - anything specific about how this role should/should not appear.
- Open threads - what is still thin.

## Interview coverage palette (deepen-role)
1. Narrative arc  2. Scope & scale  3. Key initiatives  4. Metrics
5. Tech & architecture  6. Leadership & people  7. Decisions & tradeoffs  8. Challenges & lessons
