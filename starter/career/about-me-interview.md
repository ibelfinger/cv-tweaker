# Playbook: About-me questionnaire

A repeatable, hybrid interview that builds a general identity 1-pager
(`career/about-me.md`) -- who you are as an engineer and leader. Feeds the summary
variants in `00-profile.md` and cover-letter tone. Re-runnable; the file is the
memory.

## Trigger
Plain language, e.g. "let's work on my about-me" -> run against
`career/about-me.md`.

## Style -- hybrid
Step through the seven fixed topics in order to guarantee coverage; inside each,
ask adaptive follow-ups to draw out texture. One question at a time.

## Topics
1. Engineer identity -- kind of engineer (hands-on/architect/pragmatist), strengths, what you gravitate to.
2. Leadership identity -- style, how you grow people, how you decide.
3. Dev-process beliefs -- convictions about how software should be built (TDD, trunk-based, team shape, quality vs velocity).
4. Values & motivations -- what you optimize for, what energizes you, what you won't compromise on.
5. Working style & preferences -- ambiguity tolerance, team size, remote/onsite, where you do your best work.
6. Outside work -- interests, side projects, communities.
7. What's next -- what you want in the next role (direction, not job-specific).

## Loop
1. Load & orient -- read `career/about-me.md`; find the first topic that is thin or
   listed in `## Open threads`.
2. Ask one question per topic; follow up adaptively; move to the next topic when satisfied.
3. Record honestly -- only the user's own words.
4. Wrap -- write material into the matching sections; update `## Open threads`;
   report coverage.

## Honesty rule
Everything recorded must come from the user's own words. Never fabricate
personality or beliefs.
