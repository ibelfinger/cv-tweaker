# Playbook: Per-role deepening interview

A repeatable, adaptive interview that enriches a single role's inventory file
(`career/<role>.md`) into a broad career record. Re-runnable; the role file is
the memory.

## Trigger
Plain language, e.g. "let's deepen the <role> role" -> run against
`career/<role>.md`.

## Style -- adaptive
One question at a time. Chase the signal: when an answer surfaces something meaty
(a metric, a system, a hard decision), dig with follow-ups; when a thread runs
dry, pivot. The eight dimensions below are a coverage palette to keep in view,
NOT a checklist to march through. Breadth accumulates across runs.

## Coverage palette
1. Narrative arc -- why joined, state on arrival, how it evolved, why left.
2. Scope & scale -- team size, org position, budget, systems owned, traffic/volume.
3. Key initiatives -- top accomplishments: situation -> what *you* did -> measurable result.
4. Metrics -- every quantifiable outcome (before/after, %, $, throughput, time).
5. Tech & architecture -- concrete languages, frameworks, datastores, infra, patterns.
6. Leadership & people -- hiring, mentoring, who you worked with/reported to, stakeholders.
7. Decisions & tradeoffs -- judgment calls you're proud of or that were hard.
8. Challenges & lessons -- what went wrong, what you'd do differently.

ATS keywords are derived from the above, not interviewed for.

## Loop
1. Load & orient -- read `career/<role>.md`; note rich vs thin sections and the
   `## Open threads` list; open on the most promising thin thread.
2. Ask one question; follow the signal with follow-ups; pivot when dry.
3. Record honestly -- write ONLY what the user said. No inference or embellishment.
4. Wrap (on "done" or diminishing returns) -- write new material into the matching
   sections; update `## Open threads` to reflect what's still thin; report what was
   added and what remains.

## Honesty rule
Everything recorded must come from the user's own words. If unsure, ask; never
fill gaps with assumptions.
